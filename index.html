HackPS is an AI system that answers a question most hackathon teams silently struggle with:

“Which problem statement are we actually built to win?”

Instead of assuming all teams are interchangeable, our model treats a team as a system — not just a list of skills. Team members’ backgrounds, skill overlap, and past project signals are combined to form a team synergy profile.

Hackathon problem statements are then analyzed for domain requirements, complexity, and time risk. Using ML-based ranking models, the system assigns a probability of successful execution to each statement for that specific team.

What makes the system different is explainability. Every recommendation comes with a clear breakdown showing why a problem fits or fails — whether due to skill gaps or unrealistic scope. This allows teams to make strategic decisions, not blind guesses.

Open Innovation

The Open Innovation track is the ultimate playground for creative freedom. It invites participants to identify their own challenges and build solutions without being tethered to a specific corporate prompt.

In a hackathon context, this means you can bridge disparate industries—like merging FinTech with Green Energy—using any tech stack you desire.

It rewards teams that have a strong, original vision and the ability to find a 'product-market fit' within 24-48 hours. Because there are no pre-defined constraints, the judging usually focuses on originality, technical complexity, and disruption potential.

It is the perfect choice for squads who already have a breakthrough idea they have been dying to prototype.

Problem Statement

Problem Statements are the heartbeat of focused hackathons, usually curated by industry sponsors to solve high-stakes, real-world issues.

These tracks provide a clear 'North Star,' defining the exact pain points, target users, and technical constraints you must operate within.

Success here depends on your team's ability to dive deep into a specific domain—be it cybersecurity protocols or healthcare logistics—and deliver high-utility results.

Unlike open tracks, you are competing on execution and precision rather than just the 'newness' of the idea. It’s a test of how well your team can align its technical DNA with professional requirements.
